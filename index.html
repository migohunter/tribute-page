Yoko Kanno

Eclectic Composer Extraordinaire


Personal Life

  • Born March 18th, 1964 in Sendai, Japan
  • Won multiple awards for composition contests when she was young
  • Raised Catholic and was fascinated with Christian imagery but she was never religious
  • Majored in literature at Waseda university
  • Enjoys writing and photography besides creating music
  • Speaks a little bit of English and some French in addition to Japanese.

Notable works

  • Song to Fly, first studio album with all tracks composed and conducted by Yoko
  • Space Bio Charge, album released in 2009 with her jazz band The Seatbelts
  • First anime work was arranging the ending theme for Miyazaki's movie Porco Rosso
  • Composed soundtracks for Macross Plus and The Vision of Escaflowne
  • Well known for composing the acclaimed jazz soundtrack of the 1998 anime Cowboy Bebop
  • Incorporated more techno music for the soundtrack of both seasons of Ghost in the Shell SAC
  • Visited Iceland and had some songs sung in Icelandic for her work on her most recent anime Terror in Resonance (2014)
  • Yoko's work spans to include numerous video games, movies, television shows, collaboration with other artists, and commercial music


"It was only afterwards when I started to receive feedback from fans of the series (Macross Plus) who said things like, 'I heard that song, joined the Air Force and went to Iraq' or 'I contemplated suicide while listening to Sharon Apple,' when I noticed the effect music has on people and became afraid of myself and the power of sound."

-Yoko Kanno
